lock matching and three-dimensional filtering (BM3D) is still one of the most powerful and widely used algorithms for classical image denoising methods. However, since the BM3D method uses various transformations, various threshold rules, and various statistical assumptions, it may lose some details in the image during noise reduction and may smooth it excessively. This is particularly true in areas where there is high noise in the image and fine textures are present. Deep learning-based approaches, while capable of representing different datasets, face various challenges such as requiring large amounts of data, difficulties in generalization, and instability under varying noise conditions.
